Predicting Environmental Waste Violations for the EPA

New York holds 22% of all large quantity hazardous waste generators in the U.S. — yet regulators can only inspect 3% each year. Working with the EPA and NYS Dept. of Environmental Conservation, I built a machine learning pipeline to predict which facilities are most likely to violate compliance rules, helping allocate scarce inspection resources more equitably and effectively.

Ran a grid of 282 models (Random Forest, KNN, Logistic Regression, AdaBoost) across 193 features drawn from RCRA, NYSDEC waste reports, and Census ACS data. Best model achieved ~92% precision at top 3% — far outperforming the random baseline. Included a full fairness audit on FNR disparity across high-poverty zip codes.

Models: 282  ·  Features: 193  ·  Best Precision@3%: ~92%  ·  Dataset: 9,172 LQGs · NY State · 2009–2017

Discovering Agendas in DOJ Press Releases

Used LDA topic modeling and t-SNE clustering on 13,000 Department of Justice press releases (2009–present) to surface the "expressed agendas" of each Attorney General. Revealed clear partisan shifts — notably, sharp declines in voting rights and disability cases under the last two AGs — with policy recommendations delivered to the House Judiciary Committee.

Corpus: 13,000 press releases · 57MB  ·  Topics: 10 discovered
